Disciplines United
"Psychology gives AI its purpose. Engineering gives psychology its scale."
APSYLab is an open research initiative under the non-profit AiInPsychology (AIP). We believe that the most meaningful AI systems — ones that genuinely serve people — can only be built when the disciplines converge. This lab is the infrastructure for that convergence: structured projects, shared tools, and a community fluent in both the science of mind and the science of machines.
Research

Six verticals.
One shared mission.

Each vertical brings a different lens to human-centered AI. Projects span from early-stage hypothesis to deployed tools.

01
Cognitive Load & UX
Psych students audit interfaces for attention and memory load. Engineers instrument, A/B test, and iterate. Real research, real products.
Attention HCI UX Testing
02
Mental Health Tooling
Chat-based interventions, mood tracking systems, and crisis detection pipelines built at the intersection of clinical insight and NLP.
Clinical Psych NLP Crisis Detection
03
Behavioral AI
Bias detection, persuasion analysis in LLM outputs, emotion recognition, and understanding the psychology behind model-human interaction.
Bias Detection LLM Analysis Ethics
04
Neuro-data & BCI
EEG and fMRI pipelines, sleep stage classification, attention modeling, and brain-computer interface research grounded in neuroscience.
EEG / fMRI Sleep Research BCI
05
EdPsych & EdTech
Learning curve modeling, adaptive systems, retention research. Building educational tools informed by how people actually learn and forget.
Learning Science Adaptive AI Memory
06
Human-Computer Interaction
Eye tracking studies, response latency experiments, interface perception research. Rigorous HCI with academic publication goals.
Eye Tracking Perception Experiments
Community

Two disciplines.
One workflow.

APSYLab is structured so that both psych and engineering students contribute where they're strongest — and grow where they're weakest.

If you study Psychology
You bring the "what" and "why"
  • 1Browse open project boards and post a research problem you care about
  • 2Tag your background — clinical, cognitive, neuro, social, educational
  • 3Get matched with an engineering collaborator or team
  • 4Lead the research design, literature, and evaluation criteria
  • 5Co-author outputs: papers, tools, or open datasets
If you study Engineering / CS
You bring the "how"
  • 1Browse posted problems or bring a technical idea needing a psych angle
  • 2Tag your stack — ML, NLP, systems, frontend, data engineering
  • 3Get matched with a psychology researcher or student
  • 4Lead the implementation, infrastructure, and deployment
  • 5Ship real tools with real users — not just side projects

Platform

Built for serious
interdisciplinary work

The tools and community infrastructure that make cross-discipline research actually happen.

⚑
Open Project Boards
Post a research problem, browse open ones. Like GitHub Issues but for interdisciplinary science. Problems are tagged by vertical, difficulty, and required disciplines.
◉
Role Tagging & Matching
Tag what you bring: "I speak clinical psych + statistical modeling" or "I do NLP + backend infrastructure." The system finds your complement.
◈
Reading Rooms
Shared paper discussion threads where both disciplines annotate from their lens. A clinical psychologist and an ML engineer reading the same paper see very different things — that gap is where insight lives.
↑
Mentorship Tracks
Undergrads connected with grad students and practitioners who've crossed both worlds. Structured mentorship within each research vertical.
≡
Public Lab Notebooks
Every project maintains an open research log — methodology, dead ends, partial results. Public by default. This isn't just transparency — it's how you build credibility before you publish, attract collaborators mid-project, and contribute to a culture of honest science. The notebook is the research record, not just the paper.
